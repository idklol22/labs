01 — Research Focus

Understanding neural networks from the inside out

We develop methods to decompose, analyze, and understand the computational mechanisms learned by deep neural networks.

01

Circuit Discovery

Identifying and analyzing computational subgraphs within transformer models that implement specific algorithmic behaviors, from induction heads to indirect object identification.

Neural Pathways
02

Feature Geometry

Mapping the structure of learned representations in activation space. We study superposition, polysemanticity, and the geometric organization of features across layers.

Representations
03

Scalable Interpretability

Developing automated tools and methods — sparse autoencoders, activation patching, causal tracing — that scale interpretability to frontier models.

Tooling

02 — Current Work

Active research directions

Our current projects span circuit-level analysis, dictionary learning at scale, automated interpretability pipelines, and the mathematical foundations of representation.

01

Attention Head Taxonomy

A comprehensive classification of attention head types in GPT-scale models, identifying 14 distinct functional categories across 96 layers.

Circuits
02

Sparse Autoencoder Scaling

Training dictionary learning models with 10M+ features on frontier LLMs to decompose residual stream activations into interpretable components.

Features
03

Automated Circuit Finding

Graph-based algorithms for automatically discovering minimal circuits responsible for specific model behaviors, with formal completeness guarantees.

Tools
04

Superposition & Capacity

Mathematical frameworks for understanding how models represent more features than dimensions, and the phase transitions governing feature storage.

Theory
